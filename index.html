What is Menopause?

Menopause is the time that marks the end of a women's menstrual cycles. It typically occurs in midlife, most commonly between the ages of 45 and 55 and is diagnosed after you've gone 12 months without a menstrual period.
Here are some key points about menopause:

  • Perimenopause: This phase precedes menopause and can last for several years. During perimenopause, hormone levels fluctuate, leading to irregular menstrual cycles and symptoms such as hot flashes, night sweats, mood changes, and vaginal dryness.
  • Menopause: Defined as the point when a woman has not had a menstrual period for 12 consecutive months. At this stage, ovarian function has significantly decreased, resulting in lower estrogen and progesterone levels.
  • Postmenopause: Begins after menopause and lasts for the rest of a woman's life. Symptoms such as hot flashes may continue, but generally become less frequent over time. Long-term health considerations include osteoporosis and heart disease, as lower estrogen levels can affect bone density and cardiovascular health.
  • Health considerations: Menopause is a natural process and not a disease, but it does bring about changes that can impact quality of life and overall health. Regular check-ups with healthcare providers are important to monitor and manage any potential health risks associated with aging and menopause.
