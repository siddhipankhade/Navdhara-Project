MOOD SWINGS

Most people who go through menopause won't develop a major mood disorder. But many will experience some mood problems before, during, and after menopause when hormone levels fluctuate.
These hormonal shifts can begin as early as perimenopause that is the period right before menopause.
How To Manage Mood Swings?
  • Improve Your Diet: Maintain a balanced diet to keep your body in shape and reduce stress.
  • Exercise: Engage in physical activity to release endorphins and uplift your mood.
  • Get Adequate Sleep: Prioritize good sleep hygiene to feel less stressed.
  • Try Relaxation Techniques: Actively promote relaxation through techniques like deep breathing or meditation.
  • Talk About Your Feelings: Share your emotions with someone you trust.
  • Reserve Time For Yourself: Engage in enjoyable activities as a distraction from stressors.
What Causes Mood Swings?

  • Sleep Deprivation:
    Females who have vasomotor symptoms (like hot flashes and night sweats) or have trouble sleeping may have mood changes caused by sleep deprivation. After the age of 40, your chances of sleep disturbances rise.

    If any of the following are true for you, you might be experiencing sleep deprivation:

    1. You wake up at night with night sweats:Even if your night sweats are mild, they can keep you from getting deep, restorative sleep.You might think you are getting eight hours of sleep per night, but if you never get into a deep sleep, you could still be sleep deprived.
    2. You regularly get less than seven hours of sleep a night:People are less productive and will have problems with memory and concentration if they ignore the need for sleep.
    3. You wake up thinking about problems:Stress is a major reason for sleep loss, so learning stress management techniques and ways to fall back asleep can give you more emotional stability to cope with lifeâ€™s challenges.
    4. You Snore:Snoring could be a symptom of sleep apnea. Weight gain and age can contribute to sleep apnea. Like night sweats, sleep apnea can prevent restorative sleep. If you snore and are tired during the day, you may want to consider a sleep study to see if you need treatment.
