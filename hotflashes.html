HOT FLASHES

What Is a Hot Flash?

Hot flashes are the most common symptom of perimenopause and menopause, which is the natural time when your ovaries stop releasing eggs and your period stops.
During a hot flash, you have a feeling of intense heat in your face, neck, and chest. You may also sweat large amounts and feel chilly afterward.

What Does a Hot Flash Feel Like?

Hot flashes can be different for everyone. Some people may not have symptoms that bother them, while other people have symptoms that affect their daily lives. However, common symptoms may include:

  • Feelings of intense, internal heat
  • Flushing, especially in the face, neck, and chest
  • Chills after the hot flash is over due to excessive loss of body heat
  • Feelings of anxiety

How to Stop Hot Flashes?

Hot flashes will usually go away eventually without any treatment.
Some self-care options that can help you manage your hot flashes include:

  1. Exercise Regularly: Sedentary people may have hot flashes more often than people who exercise regularly. However, do check in with yourself while you exercise, as overheating can trigger a hot flash.
  2. Loose Weight: If you're carrying extra body weight, it can make your hot flashes worse.
  3. Avoid Spicy Food: Avoid caffeinated drinks, and alcohol. These can all trigger hot flashes.
  4. Keep Cool: Dress in layers so you can take clothes off when you're too warm. Sip cold drinks when you're feeling too warm.
  5. Practice Mind-Body Therapies Like:
    • Mindfulness meditation
    • Deep breathing
    • Hypnosis
Foods That Can Help With Hot Flashes?
  1. Soy Products:
    Soy contains compounds that have been shown to help control hot flashes in some studies.
    Try eating soy-based products like:
    • Tofu
    • Soybeans or edamame
    • Soy milk
  2. Vegetables:
    • Broccoli
    • Spinach
    • Kale
    • Brussels sprouts
    • Asparagus
  3. Fruits:
    • Cucumber
    • Carrots
    • Bananas
    • Watermelon
    • Pears
    • Apples
Foods To Avoid?
  1. Coffee: The caffeine in coffee can also elevate your heart rate and cause dehydration, which may intensify those feelings.
  2. Alcohol: Alcohol can disrupt hormone balance, leading to hormonal fluctuations that may trigger or worsen hot flashes.
  3. Processed Sugar and Fats: Eating processed food tends to heighten your blood pressure, so you may see an increase in your symptoms of hot flashes.
    Avoid products like:
    • Baked Goods
    • Fast Foods
    • Fried Foods
    • Butter and Margarine
